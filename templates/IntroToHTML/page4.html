HTML Headings

3.1 Introduction to Headings

Like other forms of writting, HTML Headings are vital for organizing content, optimizing user experience and much more. By using the appropriate heading levels, web developers can create well-structured and accessible pages.

3.2 Heading Levels

HTML defines six levels of headings ranging from <h1> to <h6>. Each level indicates the importance of the heading, with <h1> being the most significant and <h6> being the least.

Note:

<h1> usually appears on the top of the page and should only be used once on a page.

<h2> Is mostly used to group content into different parts.

<h3> to <h6> are used for sub-headings with decreasing importance. An example can be seen in the image below.
